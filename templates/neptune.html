{%extends "base.html"%} {%block title%} NEPTUNE {%endblock title%} {%block img%} {%endblock img%} {%block name%} NEPTUNE {%endblock name%} {%block des1%} Neptune is the eighth and farthest known planet from the Sun in our solar system. It is classified as an ice giant and is similar in composition to Uranus. {%endblock des1%} {%block head1%} Size and Composition {%endblock head1%} {%block content1%} Neptune has a diameter of about 49,244 kilometers (30,598 miles), making it the fourth-largest planet in our solar system. It is primarily composed of hydrogen, helium, methane, and other trace gases. The presence of methane in its atmosphere gives Neptune a deep blue color. {%endblock content1%} {%block head2%} Atmosphere {%endblock head2%} {%block content2%} Neptune's thick atmosphere contains dynamic cloud systems, including prominent dark spots and bright clouds of varying sizes. The planet experiences extremely high winds, with the fastest atmospheric winds in the solar system, reaching speeds of over 1,500 miles per hour (2,400 kilometers per hour). {%endblock content2%} {%block head3%} Great Dark Spot {%endblock head3%} {%block content3%} Similar to Jupiter's Great Red Spot, Neptune has a feature called the Great Dark Spot. This massive storm system was first observed by the Voyager 2 spacecraft during its flyby in 1989. However, subsequent observations showed that the Great Dark Spot had disappeared, suggesting that Neptune's atmosphere is dynamic and ever-changing. {%endblock content3%} {%block head4%} Rings {%endblock head4%} {%block content4%} Neptune has a faint ring system, although it is not as extensive or prominent as the ring systems of Saturn. The rings are composed of dark particles, likely a mixture of water ice and other organic materials {%endblock content4%} {%block head5%} Moons {%endblock head5%} {%block content5%} Neptune has 14 known moons, with the largest moon being Triton. Triton is unique among large moons in the solar system because it has a retrograde orbit, meaning it orbits Neptune in the opposite direction of the planet's rotation. This suggests that Triton may have been captured by Neptune's gravity. {%endblock content5%} {%block head6%} Space Missions {%endblock head6%} {%block content6%} The only spacecraft to have visited Neptune is Voyager 2, which conducted a flyby in 1989. No future missions to Neptune have been planned as of my knowledge cutoff in January 2022. {%endblock content6%} {%block head7%} Magnetic Field {%endblock head7%} {%block content7%} Neptune has a strong and tilted magnetic field, which is offset from its rotational axis. The magnetic field is thought to be generated by complex processes in the planet's interior, similar to Uranus. {%endblock content7%} {%block head8%} Temperature and Conditions {%endblock head8%} {%block content8%} Neptune is a cold planet, with temperatures dropping as low as -220 degrees Celsius (-364 degrees Fahrenheit). It receives very little sunlight due to its great distance from the Sun. {%endblock content8%} {%block des2%} Neptune's dynamic atmosphere, unique features, and the mystery surrounding its changing storms make it an interesting subject of study in planetary science. Despite its distance from Earth, spacecraft observations have provided valuable insights into the characteristics of this distant ice giant. {%endblock des2%}
